What is Freethought?

© Paulo Bittencourt

“There is no greater pleasure than to be a freethinker.”— Paulo Bittencourt(Liberated from Religion)

The Aulete Dictionary defines the word dogmatism thus: “Absolute conviction, not open to criticism”. Of all thoughts, which is the only one that doesn’t tolerate any kind of criticism? That’s right: the religious thought. Freethought is, as the name says, thinking freely, that is, without dogmatism. Therefore, the thing most opposed to Freethought, since the most dogmatic, is religion.

If dogmatism is not thinking in a free way, religion is imprisonment of the mind. In fact, it’s a dictatorship: it dictates how people must think. However, of all dictatorships religion is the most perverse: the only one to threaten people, for example, with torture after death (in a lake of fire and brimstone, and simply for doubting).

A freethinker is one who doesn’t accept indubitable and unquestionable “truths”, especially the religious ones, which are imposed through divine revelation. If revelations were worth anything, all religions would have to be true. By the way, anyone can claim to have had a divine revelation, and no one will be capable of proving that it’s a delusion or a lie. Since for the freethinker intellectual honesty is above everything else, for him truth is only what is based on evidence.

The freethinker is not opposed to God existing. The freethinker is opposed to believing without evidence and accepting without questioning. If God’s existence is obvious, why don’t so many millions of people believe? Why do so many people who believe stop believing? Why are there so many religions, with completely different teachings about God? If God’s existence were obvious, there would be no need to believe, because what is obvious is not doubted. If to believe in God it’s necessary to have faith, faith itself is evidence that God doesn’t exist.

In addition to refusing to accept that life has an end and to fear of Hell, I think the great majority of religious people believe in God because they can’t stand living without knowing why the Universe exists and how life originated. However, not having answers to all questions is by no means shameful. Quite the contrary: since it’s intellectual honesty, the consciousness of not knowing is more satisfying than the illusion of knowing.

“I think it’s much more interesting to live not knowing than to have answers which might be wrong.”— Richard Feynman(Nobel Prize in Physics)

Those who accept “truths” that are not based on evidence are irrational, and in irrationality there are no criteria. People who believe, for example, in angels and demons have no rational reasons to doubt the existence of fairies and goblins, nor the morals to ridicule those who believe in vampires and werewolves. Moreover, those who think that being an atheist is illogical cannot find it illogical for someone to worship, for example, Yemoja or Vishnu. Candomblecists and Hinduists are not atheists.

Only freethinkers are truly rational people. Their skepticism does not let them be lured by any ideology. Not believing anything devoid of evidence, freethinkers are immune also to any and all kinds of superstition.

Summary
  • Mental Dictatorship: Religion is the ultimate form of dogmatism, functioning as a mental prison that dictates how people must think, uniquely punishing doubt with the threat of eternal torture.
  • Evidence Over Revelation: Freethinkers reject unquestionable “truths” and divine revelations. Since anyone can claim a revelation, intellectual honesty demands that truth must be based strictly on evidence.
  • The Flaw of Faith: Freethinkers do not oppose the idea of God, but rather believing without evidence. If God’s existence were obvious, faith would not be necessary. The need for faith suggests that God does not exist.
  • Embracing the Unknown: Many people believe out of fear of death and out of intolerance for unanswered questions. However, honestly admitting “I don’t know” is intellectually superior to clinging to comforting illusions.
  • The Trap of Irrationality: Accepting unproven claims destroys logical boundaries. People who believe in angels or demons have no rational grounds to dismiss fairies, vampires or the gods of other religions.
  • True Rationality: Freethinkers are the only truly rational people, because their skepticism prevents them from being lured by superstitions or ideologies that lack evidence.
